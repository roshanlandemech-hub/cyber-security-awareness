Common Cyber Threats

Phishing

Tricking users into revealing personal data through fake emails.

Malware

Malicious software that damages or steals data.

Ransomware

Encrypts your data until a ransom is paid.

Social Engineering

Manipulating people to share confidential information.

Protect Yourself Online

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive data.
  • Keep software and antivirus updated.
  • Always verify URLs and sources.

Real World Cybersecurity Case Studies

The WannaCry ransomware affected over 200,000 systems globally across 150 countries, encrypting user data and demanding Bitcoin payments for decryption. It targeted computers running Microsoft Windows using an exploit called EternalBlue. The NHS (UK) and major corporations like FedEx were heavily impacted. Lesson: Always update your OS and maintain regular data backups to minimize risks.

In 2019, over 540 million Facebook user records were exposed due to poorly configured cloud servers. Information such as account IDs, comments, and reactions were accessible to anyone online. The breach raised serious questions about cloud security and data privacy. Lesson: Always secure cloud databases and monitor data-sharing permissions regularly.

Equifax, one of the largest credit reporting agencies, suffered a massive data breach that exposed personal information of 147 million people. The attack was caused by an unpatched vulnerability in Apache Struts. Lesson: Keep software up-to-date, use proper encryption, and have a strong incident response plan.

This attack targeted the SolarWinds Orion software, compromising major U.S. government agencies and Fortune 500 companies. Hackers inserted malicious code into legitimate software updates, giving them backdoor access. Lesson: Regularly audit supply chains, use code integrity checks, and monitor network traffic for anomalies.

The Colonial Pipeline, a major U.S. fuel supplier, was forced to shut down operations after a ransomware attack by the DarkSide group. The company paid a $4.4 million ransom in Bitcoin to regain control. Lesson: Implement network segmentation, strong password policies, and conduct regular cyber drills.

Use Encrypted Platforms

Disable Camera/Mic Access

Revoke unnecessary app permissions to avoid spying risks.

Control Location Access
